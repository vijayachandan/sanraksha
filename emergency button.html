Emergency Health Information

Heart Attack

If you suspect a heart attack, call emergency services immediately. Symptoms may include chest pain or discomfort, shortness of breath, sweating, nausea, or lightheadedness. While waiting for emergency help, have the person sit down, stay calm, and chew on an aspirin if they are not allergic and it is available.

Stroke

If you suspect a stroke, remember the acronym FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call emergency services. Act quickly, as time is critical. Do not give the person anything to eat or drink and keep them comfortable until help arrives.

Choking

If someone is choking and cannot breathe, perform the Heimlich maneuver. Stand behind the person, make a fist with one hand, place it slightly above the navel, and thrust inward and upward until the object is expelled or the person can breathe again.
